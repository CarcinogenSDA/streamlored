# Resident Evil 2 Remake

## Overview

Resident Evil 2 (2019) is a survival horror game developed and published by Capcom. A complete reimagining of the 1998 classic, it was released January 25, 2019 for PlayStation 4, Windows, and Xbox One. The game received universal acclaim for its presentation, gameplay, and faithfulness to the original, winning the Golden Joystick Award for Game of the Year and selling over 15.8 million copies by 2025—making it the best-selling Resident Evil game.

## Story

### Setting
On the night of September 30, 1998, two months after the Mansion Incident (Resident Evil 1), Raccoon City is overrun by zombies after a strain of the T-virus contaminates the water supply.

### Plot
Rookie police officer Leon S. Kennedy arrives in Raccoon City for his first day on the job. At a gas station, he meets Claire Redfield, who is searching for her brother Chris (a S.T.A.R.S. member from RE1). The two are separated and must independently navigate the zombie-infested city, eventually converging at the Raccoon City Police Department.

### Scenario Structure
- **First Run (Scenario A)** - Initial playthrough as Leon or Claire
- **Second Run (Scenario B)** - Unlocked after completing first run, play as the other character
- The full story requires playing both scenarios
- Only the second run reveals the true ending
- Events differ between scenarios, showing what each character was doing while the other progressed

## Main Characters

### Leon S. Kennedy
- Rookie cop on his literal first day
- Driven by strong sense of justice
- Teams up with Ada Wong during his campaign
- Discovers Umbrella's corruption through the outbreak

### Claire Redfield
- Chris Redfield's younger sister
- College student and motorcycle enthusiast
- Protects Sherry Birkin throughout her campaign
- Motivated by finding her brother and protecting the innocent

### Ada Wong
- Mysterious woman who aids Leon
- Claims to be an FBI agent investigating Umbrella
- Actually a corporate spy with hidden agenda
- Seeks the G-Virus sample

### Sherry Birkin
- Daughter of William and Annette Birkin
- Lost and alone in the police station
- Claire becomes her protector
- Central to the G-Virus plot

### William Birkin
- Umbrella scientist who created the G-Virus
- Injected himself with G-Virus after being shot
- Mutates into increasingly monstrous forms throughout the game
- Main boss enemy

### Mr. X (T-103 Tyrant)
- Umbrella's bioweapon sent to retrieve G-Virus
- Relentless pursuer throughout the police station
- Cannot be killed, only temporarily downed
- Iconic fedora and trench coat design
- Attracted by gunshots and loud noises

### Supporting Characters
- **Marvin Branagh** - Wounded RPD Lieutenant who helps before succumbing to infection
- **Robert Kendo** - Gun shop owner dealing with personal tragedy
- **Ben Bertolucci** - Imprisoned journalist with dirt on Umbrella
- **HUNK** - Umbrella operative, playable in bonus mode
- **Annette Birkin** - William's wife, Sherry's mother

## Gameplay Mechanics

### Camera & Controls
- Over-the-shoulder third-person view (RE4 style)
- Free movement in any direction
- Modern control scheme compared to original's tank controls
- Photo-realistic graphics with real-time damage modeling

### Combat System
**Weapons & Ammunition**
- Limited ammo requires careful resource management
- Gunpowder crafting system to create ammunition
- Different gunpowder combinations yield different ammo types
- Leon and Claire have different weapon loadouts

**Knife Mechanics**
- Knives have durability gauges
- Break after extended use
- Can be stabbed into grabbing zombies as defense
- Must be retrieved from zombie corpses

**Defensive Items**
- Wooden boards barricade windows and doors
- Flash grenades stun enemies
- Hand grenades deal heavy damage

### Enemy Behavior
**Zombies**
- Take visible, localized damage
- Limbs can be destroyed
- Headshots not always fatal
- Can break through barricades

**Lickers**
- Blind but extremely sensitive to sound
- Can be avoided by walking slowly
- Fast and deadly if alerted

**Mr. X Encounters**
- Stalks specific areas of the game
- Attracted by gunfire
- Can follow through most doors
- Creates tension and forces route planning

### Exploration & Puzzles
- Police station serves as semi-open hub
- Collect keys and items to unlock new areas
- Classic RE puzzle design (medallions, plugs, chess pieces)
- Optional exploration yields better weapons and upgrades

### Progression System
- Items and weapons carry between saves
- Ink ribbons required for saving (Hardcore mode only)
- Hip pouch upgrades expand inventory
- Weapon parts enhance existing guns

## Game Modes

### Standard Difficulty
- Generous checkpoints
- Unlimited saves
- More resources available

### Hardcore Difficulty
- Limited saves via ink ribbons
- Fewer resources
- Enemies deal more damage
- Mr. X is more aggressive

### Bonus Modes
- **The 4th Survivor** - Play as HUNK escaping the sewers
- **The Tofu Survivor** - Play as a block of tofu with only a knife
- **Ghost Survivors** - "What if" scenarios for side characters

## Differences from Original

### Major Changes
- Third-person over-the-shoulder camera (vs fixed camera angles)
- Mr. X appears in both scenarios (vs only Scenario B originally)
- Completely redesigned RPD layout
- New voice cast and motion capture
- Realistic gore and damage system

### Story Additions
- Expanded character development
- More detailed Umbrella lore
- Enhanced Ada Wong subplot
- Orphanage section added

## Tips for New Players

### Resource Management
- Don't kill every zombie—sometimes running is better
- Save shotgun ammo for Lickers and tight groups
- Craft ammo when gunpowder types match your needs
- Hip pouches are priority upgrades

### Combat Tips
- Aim for legs to create crawlers
- Two-three headshots usually stagger zombies
- Knife downed zombies to conserve ammo
- Board up windows before Mr. X appears

### Mr. X Strategy
- Walk when possible to avoid detection
- Learn safe room locations
- He can't enter save rooms
- Plan escape routes before engaging

### Exploration
- Check every room thoroughly
- Return to previously locked areas
- Read files for puzzle hints and lore
- Map shows items you've missed
